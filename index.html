Research Focus

Bridging high-fidelity CFD simulations with sustainable energy infrastructure.

I specialize in **computational fluid dynamics (CFD)** for aerospace and mechanical systems. My work involves turbulence modeling and moving-geometry simulations (sliding/overset meshes) to analyze unconventional wind turbines. Using **OpenFOAM** and high-performance computing, I bridge the gap between component-level aerodynamics and system-level sustainability.

URANS-based CFD simulation: Wake development of a 3-blade VAWT.

Selected Projects

Track-Based VAWT

Developed CFD frameworks using sliding and overset mesh techniques to optimize blade trajectories and unsteady flow features.

VAWT Aerodynamics

URANS-based simulations of Darrieus-type turbines, focusing on torque generation, wake interaction, and dynamic stall phenomena.

HAWT Simulation

Utilizing **OpenFAST** and CFD aerodynamic modeling to study rotor performance across complex operating regimes.

Moving-Geometry SPH

Comparative studies between grid-based CFD and adaptive SPH for flapping airfoils and multi-body flow systems.
