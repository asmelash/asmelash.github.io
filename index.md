## Research Focus
My research centers on high-fidelity **computational fluid dynamics (CFD)** for aerospace, mechanical, and wind/renewable energy systems. I specialize in CFD with turbulence modeling, moving-geometry simulations (sliding and overset meshes), and aerodynamic analysis of unconventional wind turbines using **OpenFOAM** and high-performance computing workflows. My goal is to bridge component-level CFD with system-level energy and sustainability applications.

## Selected Projects

### Track-Based Vertical-Axis Wind Turbines
- Developed CFD frameworks in **OpenFOAM** using sliding and overset mesh techniques for complex blade kinematics.
- Demonstrated aerodynamic performance improvements through optimized blade trajectories and unsteady flow analysis.

### Vertical-Axis Wind Turbine Aerodynamics
- URANS-based simulations of Darrieus-type turbines under unsteady inflow conditions.
- Analysis of torque generation, wake interaction, and dynamic stall phenomena.

### Horizontal-Axis Wind Turbine Simulation
- Wind turbine analysis using **OpenFAST** and CFD-based aerodynamic modeling.
- Studied rotor performance and flow structures across operating regimes.

### Complex Moving-Geometry CFD & SPH
- Comparative studies between **OpenFOAM** and **adaptive SPH** for geometrically complex moving bodies.
- Applications to flapping airfoils and multi-body flow systems.
